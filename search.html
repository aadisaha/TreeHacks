SEARCH: "parkinson's disease"

Summary

Parkinson's disease is a brain disorder that causes unintended or uncontrollable movements, such as shaking, stiffness, and difficulty with balance and coordination. The main therapy for Parkinson's is levodopa, usually taken with another medication called carbidopa.

Levodopa is the most effective medication for treating the motor symptoms of Parkinson's disease. It is usually taken with another medication called carbidopa, which helps protect levodopa from early conversion to dopamine outside the brain. Common side effects include nausea, vomiting, dizziness, and headache. The pros of using this drug include its effectiveness in treating the motor symptoms of PD, while the cons include potential side effects. Alternative uses for levodopa include treating restless leg syndrome and depression.
